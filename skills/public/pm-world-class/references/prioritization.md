# Prioritization

Use simple methods that make tradeoffs legible.

## Fast ranking method

Score each item 1-5 on:
- user pain
- business impact
- strategic leverage
- confidence
- effort (reverse-scored)
- urgency / timing

Then explain the ranking in plain English. Do not hide behind the math.

## RICE (good for broad feature sets)
- Reach
- Impact
- Confidence
- Effort

Use when comparing many candidate bets. Keep inputs rough; avoid fake precision.

## Cost of delay (good for sequencing)
Estimate what waiting costs in:
- revenue
- learning
- risk exposure
- customer trust

Use when several items are valuable but timing differs.

## Strategic fit filter
Ask:
- does this strengthen the core product/system?
- does this unlock future capabilities?
- does this reduce ongoing operational drag?
- does this help a priority customer segment?

## Good prioritization behavior
- say no clearly
- separate “important” from “important now”
- identify prerequisite work
- mention confidence level
- note what evidence would change the ranking
