# Examples

## Example 1: Weak stakeholder update

"The project is progressing well. The team worked hard this week and we are making good progress. There are some risks we are monitoring and we expect to continue moving forward."

## Better

- **Status:** At risk
- **What changed:** API dependency slipped 5 business days due to vendor auth issues.
- **Current focus:** Unblocking auth integration and preserving pilot scope.
- **Risk:** If unresolved by Thursday, pilot moves from May 20 to May 27.
- **Decision needed:** Approve temporary manual import fallback by Wednesday.
- **Next milestone:** End-to-end staging validation Friday.

Why better: concrete, scannable, decision-oriented.

## Example 2: Weak strategy statement

"We should invest in onboarding because it is strategically important and will improve the user experience."

## Better

"Recommend prioritizing onboarding simplification this quarter because 38% of trial users fail at step 2, making activation the highest-leverage conversion constraint. This delays lower-impact reporting work but is the fastest path to improving paid conversion before Q3 acquisition spend increases."

Why better: evidence, tradeoff, timing.

## Example 3: Weak PRD goal

"Make notifications better for users."

## Better

"Reduce time-to-first-response for marketplace sellers from 4.2 hours to under 1 hour by introducing priority notification routing for high-intent buyer messages."

Why better: specific user, measurable outcome, clear problem.
