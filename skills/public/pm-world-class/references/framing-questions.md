# Framing Questions

Use these when the request is ambiguous. Ask only the minimum set needed.

## Problem clarity
- What decision needs to be made?
- What problem is actually hurting users or the business?
- What changes if this is solved?
- What happens if nothing is done?

## Scope
- Who is the primary user or stakeholder?
- What is explicitly in scope?
- What is explicitly out of scope?
- What deadline or timing constraint matters?

## Success
- How will success be measured?
- What metric or behavior should change?
- What does a “good enough” first version look like?

## Constraints
- What constraints are fixed: time, people, budget, compliance, architecture?
- What dependencies could block execution?
- What assumptions are currently unverified?

## Tradeoffs
- What are we optimizing for?
- What are we willing to give up?
- What alternative paths were considered?

## Stakeholder alignment
- Who approves this?
- Who executes it?
- Who could block it?
- Who needs to be informed but not asked?

## Launch / execution
- What must be true before launch?
- What can wait until after launch?
- What is the rollback or contingency plan?
